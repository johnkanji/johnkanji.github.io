John Kanji

computer graphics student, serial amateurist.
jkanji@dgp.toronto.edu

Hello, I am a computer graphics PhD student studying at the Dynamic Graphics Project, University of Toronto where my advisor is Prof. David Levin. My research interests are a mix of graphics, deep learning, vision, and a little human-computer interaction. I like to work on problems in computer animation, and generally go about that using geometric and data-driven approaches. I have also pitched in on projects in physical simulation and sometimes tinker with computational fabrication. Before coming to Toronto I did my Bachelors degree at the University of York, United Kingdom where my undergraduate research project was an FPGA implementation of a gyroscopic sensor-fusion algorithm.
